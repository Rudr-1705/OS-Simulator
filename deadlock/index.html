Key Concepts

Deadlock
A situation where processes are blocked forever, waiting for resources held by each other.
Mutual Exclusion
At least one resource must be held in a non-sharable mode.
Hold and Wait
A process holding at least one resource is waiting to acquire additional resources held by others.
No Preemption
Resources cannot be forcibly removed from the processes holding them.
Circular Wait
A closed chain of processes exists, such that each process holds at least one resource needed by the next process in the chain.
Safe State
A state where the system can allocate resources to each process in some order and still avoid deadlock.

Deadlock Handling Strategies

StrategyDescriptionAlgorithm example
PreventionEnsure at least one of the 4 necessary conditions never holds.Resource ordering, conservative allocation
AvoidanceDynamically check resource requests to ensure safe state.Banker's Algorithm
DetectionAllow deadlock, detect it, and recover.Wait-for / RAG Cycle detection
IgnoranceIgnore the problem completely.Ostrich Algorithm (common in many OS)
